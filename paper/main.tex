\begin{abstract}
\noindent\textbf{Background.}
Conventional diversity metrics are insensitive to the loop-level
structure of microbial co-occurrence networks.
Persistent homology, from topological data analysis (\tda), tracks how
loops and other higher-order features form and dissolve across all
correlation thresholds simultaneously.

\noindent\textbf{Methods.}
We applied persistent homology to Spearman co-occurrence networks from
3,409 American Gut Project (\agp) stool samples (\clr-transformed;
top-80 prevalent taxa).
A paired bootstrap (200 iterations, $n = 100$/group) yielded distributions
of six \Hone\ scalar features.
Significance was assessed via sign-flip permutation (500 shuffles; primary)
and Wilcoxon signed-rank (confirmatory), with Benjamini--Hochberg correction
across 18 tests per subset.
All analyses were repeated in age/sex/BMI-matched subsamples and at
taxon-set sizes $N \in \{50, 80, 120\}$.

\noindent\textbf{Results.}
Inflammatory bowel disease (\ibd; $n = 165$ vs.\ $n = 3{,}084$ healthy)
was associated with systematically lower topological complexity across all
six features (Cohen's $d = 0.75$--$2.38$; $p_{\text{FDR}} < 0.005$ at all
taxa sizes), fully preserved after covariate matching.
Plant-based diet showed moderately longer-lived loops (mean lifetime
$d = 0.65$; $p_{\text{FDR}} = 0.004$); antibiotic recency produced limited,
matching-dependent signal.

\noindent\textbf{Conclusions.}
\Ibd-associated microbiomes are topologically simpler than healthy controls,
suggesting that the disease state reshapes co-occurrence architecture beyond
what diversity indices capture.
Persistent homology provides a structurally informative complement to
existing microbiome metrics.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} persistent homology, gut microbiome, inflammatory bowel
disease, co-occurrence networks, topological data analysis, American Gut
Project, bootstrap, FDR
\end{abstract}

\section*{Data and Code Availability}

All code, download scripts, result CSVs, and figure generation notebooks
are publicly available at
\url{https://github.com/adam-jfkhs/Microbiome-TDA}.
Primary analysis entry points are
\texttt{scripts/run\_agp\_bootstrap\_v2.py} (main bootstrap pipeline)
and \texttt{scripts/run\_taxa\_sensitivity.py} (taxon-set sensitivity analysis).
The American Gut Project (AGP) data used in this study are publicly
available via the European Nucleotide Archive (accession ERP012803) and
through the redbiom query interface (\url{https://redbiom.readthedocs.io}).
The IBDMDB validation data are available at \url{https://ibdmdb.org}.
