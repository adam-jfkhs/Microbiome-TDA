\begin{abstract}
\noindent\textbf{Background.}
Conventional diversity metrics are insensitive to the loop-level
structure of microbial co-occurrence networks.
Persistent homology, from topological data analysis (\tda), tracks how
loops and other higher-order features form and dissolve across all
correlation thresholds simultaneously.

\noindent\textbf{Methods.}
We applied persistent homology to Spearman co-occurrence networks from
3,409 American Gut Project (\agp) stool samples (\clr-transformed;
top-80 prevalent taxa).
A paired bootstrap (200 iterations, $n = 100$/group) yielded distributions
of six \Hone\ scalar features.
Significance was assessed via sign-flip permutation (500 shuffles; primary)
and Wilcoxon signed-rank (confirmatory), with Benjamini--Hochberg correction
across 18 tests per subset.
All analyses were repeated in age/sex/BMI-matched subsamples and at
taxon-set sizes $N \in \{50, 80, 120\}$.
The \agp\ finding was independently validated in the IBDMDB multi-omics
cohort and grounded against fecal calprotectin.
A leave-one-out topological attribution identified per-taxon contributions
to loop structure.
Classification benchmarking compared topological features against Shannon
diversity under 5-fold cross-validation.

\noindent\textbf{Results.}
\Ibd\ ($n = 165$ vs.\ $n = 3{,}084$ healthy) was associated with
systematically lower topological complexity across all six features
(Cohen's $|d| = 0.75$--$2.38$; $p_{\text{FDR}} < 0.005$ at all taxa sizes),
fully preserved after covariate matching.
In IBDMDB (cross-platform, WGS), a one-sample-per-subject sensitivity check
reveals that the full-dataset IBD vs.\ non-IBD comparison is driven by
overrepresentation of active-disease timepoints; the within-IBD calprotectin
comparison, which is unaffected by this sampling bias, confirms that loop
structure tracks a continuous inflammation biomarker.
Leave-one-out attribution identified \textit{Oscillospira} and
\textit{Blautia} as the dominant loop anchors in healthy \agp\ networks,
with cross-dataset replication of \textit{Bacteroides}-clade importance in
IBDMDB.
An extended classification benchmark against $\alpha$- and $\beta$-diversity
established that Aitchison-PCoA (phylogeny-free compositional $\beta$-diversity)
is the strongest single predictor (AUC $= 0.734$, logistic regression), while
topology scalars outperform Shannon under random forest and provide mechanistic
interpretability that distance-based embeddings do not supply.
Plant-based diet showed moderately longer-lived loops ($d = 0.65$,
$p_{\text{FDR}} = 0.004$); antibiotic recency produced limited signal.

\noindent\textbf{Conclusions.}
\Ibd-associated microbiomes are topologically simpler than healthy controls
across two independent cohorts and two sequencing technologies, and this
simplification tracks a clinical inflammation biomarker.
Persistent homology provides a structurally informative complement to
existing microbiome metrics, with taxon-level attribution bridging
topological statistics and named biological interactions.

\smallskip\noindent
\textbf{Keywords:} persistent homology, gut microbiome, inflammatory bowel
disease, co-occurrence networks, topological data analysis, American Gut
Project, IBDMDB, calprotectin, leave-one-out attribution, bootstrap, FDR
\end{abstract}

%─── S2: Persistence diagrams ───────────────────────────────────────────────
\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/agp_persistence_diagrams.png}
  \caption{%
    \textbf{Supplementary Figure~S2: Representative \Hone\ persistence
    diagrams for AGP healthy and IBD groups.}
    Each point represents a single topological feature (a co-occurrence
    loop) born at correlation distance $b$ (x-axis) and dying at
    distance $d$ (y-axis).
    Longer-lived features (larger $d - b$) lie further above the diagonal.
    Healthy microbiomes (blue) accumulate more features and have greater
    total off-diagonal mass than IBD microbiomes (red), visually
    confirming the loop-collapse signal quantified in the scalar
    features.
    Diagrams were computed by Ripser on the Vietoris--Rips filtration of
    the CLR-Spearman distance matrix for a representative bootstrap
    iteration at $N = 80$ taxa.
  }

\end{figure}

    \textbf{Supplementary Figure~S3: Betti-1 curves across the Vietoris--Rips
