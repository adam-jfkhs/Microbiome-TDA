\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%=============================================================================

\subsection{Loop collapse as a structural correlate of IBD dysbiosis}

The scale of the \ibd\ finding warrants careful reading before
biological interpretation.
Effect sizes spanning $d = 0.75$--$2.38$ are unusual in microbiome
research; Shannon diversity differences between \ibd\ and healthy cohorts
typically reach $d \approx 0.3$--$0.6$.
The amplification observed here reflects several features of the
topology-via-bootstrap approach.
First, persistent homology integrates correlation structure across all
taxa simultaneously: a perturbation affecting five co-occurring taxa
simultaneously depresses loop count, loop lifetime, and loop density
together, whereas alpha diversity registers only additive taxon loss.
Second, the paired bootstrap harvests the typical IBD--healthy contrast
over 200 independent draws, so the observed $d$ reflects the expected
contrast between one randomly drawn patient panel and one randomly drawn
healthy panel, rather than a contrast between two centralized estimates.
Under this framing, a large $d$ implies that the topological gap is
visible at virtually any randomly selected subsample, not merely in the
aggregate.

The direction of the finding is consistent with known IBD compositional
pathology.
Butyrate-producing Firmicutes (\textit{Faecalibacterium},
\textit{Roseburia}, \textit{Lachnospiraceae}) occupy dense, multi-partner
co-occurrence neighborhoods in healthy gut networks; their depletion in
\ibd\ would be expected to dissolve the loops anchored around
them~\citep{gevers2014treatment,halfvarson2017dynamics}.
Whether these particular taxa drive the loop collapse, or whether the
entire gram-positive anaerobe compartment contracts uniformly, is a
question amenable to taxon-attribution analysis (see Section~\ref{subsec:limitations}).

A more mechanistic reading is that the healthy gut co-occurrence graph
approximates a modular, redundant architecture: butyrate producers and
fiber-degrading specialists form mutually reinforcing exchange loops in
which each partner both consumes the metabolic byproducts of others and
releases substrates that sustain them.
Persistent loops in the $H_1$ sense require at least three taxa forming
a correlation triangle strong enough to survive across a wide filtration
range; the co-occurrence architecture of butyrate producers plausibly
satisfies this criterion.
When these taxa are depleted or become numerically erratic under
\ibd-associated inflammation, the triangles dissolve before they
consolidate, producing a persistence diagram with lower total mass and
shorter feature lifetimes.
What remains is not necessarily a sparser network in the edge-count
sense---Proteobacterial blooms can increase certain pairwise correlations
in \ibd~\citep{halfvarson2017dynamics}---but a topologically impoverished
one: shorter-lived, less reinforced cycles.
This interpretation predicts that the Proteobacterial compartment, while
elevated in \ibd, does not substitute structurally for the anaerobic
Firmicutes it displaces, a prediction testable via the loop attribution
analysis outlined above.

These structural patterns are consistent with known \ibd-associated
depletion of butyrate producers, which are mechanistically linked to the
TGF-$\beta$/Treg regulatory axis.
Butyrate---the principal metabolic output of \textit{Faecalibacterium} and
\textit{Roseburia}---suppresses NF-$\kappa$B activity and acts as an HDAC
inhibitor, promoting Foxp3$^+$ regulatory T~cell (Treg) differentiation
through TGF-$\beta$ signaling~\citep{gevers2014treatment}.
When the taxa that anchor co-occurrence loops are depleted, one would
expect butyrate supply to fall, Treg populations to contract, and the
cytokine balance to shift toward pro-inflammatory dominance by
TNF-$\alpha$, IL-6, and IL-17.
Our topological features do not measure this axis directly, but the
concordance is suggestive: a microbiome in which butyrate-producer
co-occurrence loops have collapsed is also one in which TGF-$\beta$-dependent
immune regulation is likely compromised.
Whether total persistence or mean loop lifetime correlates with circulating
cytokine concentrations is directly testable in any cohort that pairs
microbiome sequencing with immunological profiling.

\subsection{Structural reorganization versus abundance enrichment in diet}

The bifurcated diet signal (fewer total loops, longer-lived individual
loops for plant-based eaters) resists a simple narrative.
It is not that plant-based microbiomes are topologically richer in an
unqualified sense; they appear to support a different \emph{architecture}:
a sparser set of co-occurrence partnerships, but ones with more robust,
threshold-spanning correlations.
One mechanistic hypothesis consistent with this pattern is dietary
fibre-driven selection: high-fibre diets enforce strong metabolic
dependencies among a constrained set of fibre-degrading consortia
(principally \textit{Prevotella}, \textit{Ruminococcus}, and
\textit{Bifidobacterium} genera), producing persistent loops, while
reducing the proliferation of opportunistic taxa whose co-occurrences
are numerous but shallow~\citep{louis2017formation}.
This is speculative; validating it would require superimposing fibre
intake quantification on loop attribution.

The partial attenuation of the diet signal after matching (3/6 features
surviving FDR) counsels against strong claims.
It is plausible that a fraction of the full-group diet effect is
sociological, given that plant-based eaters in \agp\ are skewed toward higher
education, younger age, and metropolitan residence, rather than purely
physiological in origin.
The three features that do survive matching (mean lifetime, max
lifetime, max Betti-1) are precisely the ones reflecting loop durability,
which are less susceptible to demographic covariates than loop count.

\subsection{The antibiotic null}

That a 6-month antibiotic history window produces no coherent topological
signal after matching is not surprising, and should not be over-interpreted
as evidence that antibiotics are topologically inert.
The exposure category aggregates single-course amoxicillin prescriptions,
prolonged prophylactic macrolide regimens, and fluoroquinolone treatment:
perturbations that differ by orders of magnitude in their ecological
destructiveness~\citep{petersen2017defining}.
A better-powered analysis would stratify by antibiotic class and quantify
time since last exposure as a continuous variable.

\subsection{Relation to prior topological analyses of microbiome data}

The deployment of persistent homology in microbiology is recent and sparse.
Cam\`{a}ra and colleagues~\citep{camara2017topological} used $H_0$ and $H_1$
features to distinguish gut microbiome states in longitudinal infant data,
identifying topological transitions that co-occurred with dietary shifts at
weaning.
That work operated on a single cohort of twelve infants with dense temporal
sampling, a regime that favors trajectory-level topology rather than
group-level contrast.
Our design inverts this: large cross-sectional $n$, sparse metadata, no
temporal structure, but high statistical power for detecting mean differences
between clinically defined groups.
The two approaches are complementary rather than competing.

More broadly, TDA has been applied to protein contact networks to predict
folding stability~\citep{wasserman2018topological}, to neuroimaging data to
map functional connectivity~\citep{bubenik2015statistical}, and to materials
microstructure to predict fracture~\citep{ghrist2008barcodes}.
In each domain, the practical justification for persistent homology over
graph-theoretic alternatives is the same as here: the features of interest
are not expressible as functions of individual edges, but emerge from the
collective geometry of multi-node motifs that thresholding-based networks
miss or misrepresent.
The amplified effect sizes we observe relative to conventional alpha-diversity
are consistent with this general principle: topological features aggregate
information from many pairwise correlations simultaneously, producing a more
sensitive readout of network-wide reorganization.

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{subsec:limitations}

\paragraph{Cross-sectional topology.}
All \agp\ records are single-timepoint observations.
The causal directionality (whether topological simplification precedes \ibd\
onset, coincides with active inflammation, or lingers through clinical
remission) cannot be resolved without longitudinal sampling.

\paragraph{ASV-level resolution.}
The pipeline operates on Deblur ASVs without genus-level aggregation.
Strain-level co-occurrence heterogeneity within named genera may both inflate
and obscure loop structure, and interoperability with genus-level literature
is impaired.

\paragraph{Limited diversity-metric benchmark.}
We report a Shannon $\alpha$-diversity comparison (Section~\ref{subsec:ibd_results})
showing that topological features yield 1.5--4.7$\times$ larger effect sizes
than Shannon $d = 0.51$.
However, we did not assess UniFrac $\beta$-diversity, nor did we test whether
topological features explain variance in clinical outcomes \emph{beyond} what
simpler metrics already capture (e.g., via classification AUC comparison).
This is the most practically important open question.

\paragraph{Single cohort.}
All results originate from a self-recruited citizen-science population with
attendant ascertainment biases.
The \ibd\ group may be systematically different from clinical populations in
disease severity, medication status, and dietary self-reporting accuracy.
Independent replication in a cohort with clinical metadata (endoscopy scores,
calprotectin levels, treatment records) is required before any clinical
utility claim can be made.

\paragraph{Loop attribution absent.}
We report aggregate topological statistics but do not identify which taxon
pairs anchor the detected loops.
This is a fundamental gap between a statistical claim (IBD has topologically
simpler networks) and a biological claim (specific cross-feeding partnerships
are disrupted).
Attribution analysis, such as identifying taxa whose removal from the network
most rapidly reduces $\beta_1(\varepsilon)$, is the logical next step.

\paragraph{No inflammatory biomarker correlation.}
The \agp\ dataset contains no measurements of circulating cytokines,
fecal calprotectin, or neuroendocrine hormones.
The TGF-$\beta$/Treg mechanism outlined above is therefore an
interpretation, not a result.
Of additional interest is the melanocortin axis:
$\alpha$-melanocyte-stimulating hormone ($\alpha$-MSH), derived from
hypothalamic POMC neurons, exerts anti-inflammatory effects on mucosal
macrophages via MC1R and MC3R, suppressing NF-$\kappa$B and dampening
the very cytokines elevated in \ibd.
In dysbiosis, increased intestinal permeability can allow microbial
products (LPS, peptidoglycans) to enter systemic circulation and
suppress POMC signaling---potentially reducing $\alpha$-MSH tone and
thereby withdrawing one anti-inflammatory brake on mucosal innate
immunity.
Whether the topological simplification we observe correlates with
disrupted melanocortin signaling is a question that cohorts combining
microbiome profiling with plasma neuropeptide measurements could answer.

\paragraph{Bootstrap non-independence.}
The 200 paired iterations are constructed from overlapping samples (a sample
from the IBD group can appear in multiple iterations), so the deltas
$\delta^{(1)}, \ldots, \delta^{(200)}$ are not strictly independent.
The sign-flip permutation operates on the same correlated set of deltas
and thereby conserves this correlation structure under the null, which is
appropriate.
However, formal correction for the reduced effective sample size was not
applied to the Wilcoxon test, which assumes independence.
This is one reason why Wilcoxon results are treated as secondary.
