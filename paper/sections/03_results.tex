\subsection{IBD is associated with systematic attenuation of co-occurrence loop structure}
\label{subsec:ibd_results}

The paired bootstrap distributions for the IBD comparison are shown in
Figure~\ref{fig:ibd_bootstrap}.
Every one of the six scalar features exhibits a delta distribution shifted
leftward from zero, indicating that IBD microbiomes systematically generate
co-occurrence networks with fewer, shorter-lived, and less energetically
diverse loops than their healthy counterparts.
No feature was directionally equivocal.

  }

Numerical results appear in Table~\ref{tab:ibd_results}.
The three features with the largest effect sizes are total persistence
($d = -2.28$), max Betti-1 ($d = -2.07$), and mean lifetime ($d = -1.65$),
indicating that the healthy microbiome sustains roughly twice the cumulative
topological weight, twice the peak cycle density, and 42\% longer average
cycle lifetimes than the \ibd\ microbiome.
The permutation floor of $p = 0.002$ (the smallest achievable at
500 permutations; $\lfloor 1/501 \rfloor = 0.002$) was attained by all
six features, and Wilcoxon $p$-values were effectively zero across the
board: two independent lines of evidence converging on the same conclusion.

Covariate matching left the picture unaltered: all six features remained
FDR-significant in the matched subsample ($n_A = 135$, $n_B = 404$), and
effect sizes were numerically indistinguishable from the full-group estimates
(matched total persistence $d = -2.22$; matched max Betti-1 $d = -2.32$).

\paragraph{Comparison with Shannon $\alpha$-diversity.}
To contextualize these effect sizes, we computed Shannon diversity for
every sample in the same filtered dataset.
IBD samples showed lower Shannon entropy than healthy controls (mean
$3.24 \pm 0.75$ vs.\ $3.61 \pm 0.73$; Mann--Whitney
$p = 5.4 \times 10^{-12}$; Cohen's $|d| = 0.51$, 95\% bootstrap CI
$[0.36,\, 0.67]$).
This confirms that conventional $\alpha$-diversity detects the IBD signal,
but at roughly one-quarter to one-third the effect size of the topological
features ($|d| = 0.75$--$2.38$).
The amplification is consistent with the expectation that persistent
homology aggregates multi-taxon correlation structure that single-sample
diversity indices, by construction, cannot access.

For geometric intuition, Figure~\ref{fig:ibd_tda} displays persistence
diagrams and Betti-1 curves from a representative single-pass comparison;
the sparser diagram and attenuated Betti-1 peak for the IBD group
corroborate the bootstrap evidence at the level of individual network topology.

\subsection{Plant-based diet: fewer but more tenacious co-occurrence cycles}
\label{subsec:diet_results}

The plant-based vs.\ omnivore comparison revealed a more nuanced topological
signature than the IBD contrast (Figure~\ref{fig:diet_bootstrap}).

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/agp_diet_v2.png}
  \caption{%
    \textbf{Paired bootstrap delta distributions: Plant-based vs.\ Omnivore,
    $N = 80$ taxa.}
    Red = full groups ($n_A = 367$, $n_B = 2{,}747$);
    green = matched subset ($n_A = 308$, $n_B = 922$).
    H1 count shows a negative delta (plant-based eaters generate
    \emph{fewer} total loops), while mean lifetime, max lifetime, and
    max Betti-1 are positive (the loops they do form are more persistent
    and reach higher simultaneous densities).
    This bifurcated pattern (topological contraction in count, expansion
    in durability) suggests dietary restructuring of the co-occurrence
    landscape rather than simple enrichment or depletion.
  }
  \label{fig:diet_bootstrap}
\end{figure}

Five of six features were FDR-significant in full-group analyses: mean
lifetime ($d = +0.65$, $p_\text{FDR} = 0.004$), max Betti-1
($d = +0.46$, $p_\text{FDR} = 0.004$), total persistence
($d = +0.22$, $p_\text{FDR} = 0.030$), H1 count ($d = -0.34$,
$p_\text{FDR} = 0.007$), and H1 entropy ($d = -0.23$,
$p_\text{FDR} = 0.029$).
After matching, three features survived correction (mean lifetime,
max lifetime, max Betti-1), signalling partial but not total confounding
by demographic covariates.

\subsection{Antibiotic recency: an inconclusive topological signal}
\label{subsec:abx_results}

Recent antibiotic exposure ($\leq$6 months, $n = 590$) versus absence
of use in the preceding year ($n = 2{,}271$) produced inconsistent results.
In full-group analyses at $N = 80$, only max Betti-1 cleared FDR
($d = -0.39$, $p_\text{FDR} = 0.004$); in matched analyses at $N = 80$,
zero features survived correction.
At $N = 50$ and $N = 120$, between two and three features reached FDR
significance in matched analyses, but direction and magnitude were not
stable across sizes.
We decline to characterize this as a genuine topological effect.
The exposure definition (any antibiotic of any class within a 6-month window)
is almost certainly too heterogeneous to isolate a topological
signature; time-since-cessation, antibiotic class, and treatment duration
are all uncontrolled.

\subsection{Independent validation in IBDMDB: replication and biomarker grounding}
\label{subsec:ibdmdb}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

To address the single-cohort limitation of the \agp\ analysis, we replicated
the bootstrap pipeline on the IBDMDB multi-omics dataset~\citep{lloydprice2019multi},
a clinical longitudinal study of 106 subjects (80 \ibd\ [CD+UC], 26 non-\ibd)
followed for approximately one year.
We used 1{,}338 longitudinal metagenomics samples in total
(599 Crohn's disease, 375 UC, and 364 non-\ibd\ timepoints;
the parenthetical figures are sample counts, not subject counts)
with species-level \textsc{MetaPhlAn}2 abundances; the same \clr\ transform
and global top-80 taxa selection was applied.
Bootstrap subsampling used $n = 60$ per group per iteration.
Because IBDMDB is a longitudinal study, multiple timepoint samples from the
same subject are present in the dataset; no within-subject deduplication was
applied for the primary analysis, so individual bootstrap draws may include
repeated observations from the same participant.
Section~\ref{subsubsec:ibdmdb_1ps} below reports the one-sample-per-subject
sensitivity check, which reveals an important caveat for the IBD vs.\ non-IBD
comparison.
IBDMDB differs from \agp\ on every methodological axis: sequencing platform
(whole-genome shotgun vs.\ 16S amplicon), bioinformatics pipeline, study
design (clinical vs.\ citizen-science), and population (recruited IBD patients
vs.\ self-reported community participants).

\subsubsection*{IBD vs.\ non-IBD comparison: full-dataset results}
\label{subsubsec:ibdmdb_full}

Table~\ref{tab:ibdmdb_results} reports the primary IBD vs.\ non-IBD comparison
and the Crohn's-only and UC-only breakdowns in the \emph{full} longitudinal
dataset (all 1,338 timepoints, no deduplication).
All six features are FDR-significant ($p_\text{FDR} = 0.003$ at the
permutation floor) with effect sizes ranging from $d = -0.52$ (max Betti-1) to
$d = -1.46$ (total persistence); direction is uniformly negative---IBD
microbiomes generate fewer, shorter, and less energetically diverse
co-occurrence loops than non-IBD controls.

Breaking down by diagnosis subtype reveals an informative asymmetry.
Crohn's disease produces the strongest signal: all six features are
FDR-significant with $d$ ranging from $-0.56$ to $-1.50$.
Ulcerative colitis shows a substantially weaker but still detectable pattern:
only two of six features survive FDR correction (total persistence
$d = -0.28$, $p_\text{FDR} = 0.010$; mean lifetime $d = -0.22$,
$p_\text{FDR} = 0.040$).
This CD--UC disparity is consistent with the broader literature: Crohn's
disease involves deeper and more diffuse microbial disruption than
ulcerative colitis, which is confined to the colon and can present with
near-normal microbiome composition during clinical
remission~\citep{halfvarson2017dynamics,gevers2014treatment}.

\subsubsection*{One-sample-per-subject sensitivity check}
\label{subsubsec:ibdmdb_1ps}

To assess whether the full-dataset IBD vs.\ non-IBD comparison is an artefact
of pseudoreplication, we re-ran the primary \texttt{ibd\_vs\_nonibd} bootstrap
using exactly one randomly selected timepoint per participant (seed-fixed for
reproducibility): 974 IBD timepoints → 80 subjects; 364 non-IBD timepoints →
26 subjects; bootstrap subsample $n = 26$ (the limiting group size) per
iteration.

The result is striking: \textbf{the direction of effect reverses for 5 of 6
features} (Table~\ref{tab:ibdmdb_1ps}).
Where the full dataset shows IBD below non-IBD on every feature ($d = -0.52$
to $-1.46$), the one-sample-per-subject analysis shows IBD \emph{above}
non-IBD on five features ($d = +0.61$ to $+1.22$, all at the minimum
permutation $p = 0.002$).
Only max Betti-1 is non-significant ($d = +0.13$, $p = 0.21$).

This reversal exposes a sampling-frequency bias inherent to IBDMDB's
longitudinal design.
IBDMDB subjects with more active disease are sampled at higher frequency
(mean 12.2 timepoints per subject, range 3--25); subjects who visited the
clinic more often (and thus contributed more samples) tend to be those with
more severe or refractory disease---precisely the individuals with the lowest
topological complexity.
The full-dataset bootstrap therefore overweights active-disease timepoints,
pulling the IBD mean down relative to what would be observed at a single
randomly selected visit.
When each subject contributes equally (one timepoint), IBD subjects' mean
h$_1$ count is 40.3 vs.\ 38.0 for non-IBD controls---the opposite direction
from the full-dataset result (35.5 vs.\ 40.8).

Two further caveats apply to this comparison.
First, with only 26 non-IBD subjects (all included in every bootstrap
draw at $n = 26$), the non-IBD mean is not resampled and the test is
underpowered for detecting departures from the full-dataset estimate.
Second, IBDMDB's non-IBD controls are recruited from the same clinical
settings as IBD patients (potentially family members or clinic staff),
which may make them an unrepresentative healthy comparison group relative to
the community-recruited \agp\ cohort.

\paragraph{Implications for the IBDMDB validation.}
The one-sample-per-subject check demonstrates that the IBDMDB IBD vs.\
non-IBD topological comparison is \textbf{not a robust cross-cohort
replication} of the \agp\ finding: it is sensitive to whether subjects
are weighted by their number of clinic visits.
The more interpretable IBDMDB evidence comes from the within-IBD biomarker
comparisons (Section below), which compare timepoints drawn from the
\emph{same} subjects and are therefore unaffected by between-subject sampling
frequency.
The primary cross-sectional \agp\ finding---each subject contributes exactly
one sample---remains unaffected by this issue.

\subsubsection*{Biomarker grounding: calprotectin and clinical activity scores}

A central open question is whether topological features correlate with
direct measurements of mucosal inflammation rather than merely with the
categorical \ibd\ label.
IBDMDB provides fecal calprotectin for 456 \textsc{mgx} samples and
disease activity indices for the majority of subjects.
Splitting on a standard clinical threshold
($\geq 250\,\mu\text{g/g}$: active mucosal inflammation; $<50\,\mu\text{g/g}$:
quiescent) yields $n_\text{high} = 60$ and $n_\text{low} = 189$ samples.

Table~\ref{tab:ibdmdb_biomarkers} reports the calprotectin and activity-score
comparisons.
The calprotectin analysis reveals a pattern that is directionally more complex
than the simple IBD--healthy contrast.
High-calprotectin samples show \emph{fewer} co-occurrence loops overall
(H1 count $d = -1.27^\star$) but \emph{longer-lived} individual loops
(mean lifetime $d = +3.51^\star$; max lifetime $d = +1.98^\star$; total
persistence $d = +1.55^\star$).
This pattern---topological contraction in breadth, expansion in depth---is
consistent with the hypothesis that acute mucosal inflammation selects for
a small, structurally robust co-occurrence cluster (plausibly dominated by
Proteobacteria and \textit{Enterobacteriaceae}) while dismantling the broader
anaerobic cross-feeding network: the surviving loops are fewer but more
persistent because they are anchored by stable, inflammation-associated
co-occurrence relationships rather than the fragile, metabolically redundant
partnerships of the healthy state.
Note that with $n_\text{high} = 60$ and bootstrap subsampling at $n = 60$
per iteration, the high-calprotectin group is sampled near-exhaustively on
each draw (sampling without replacement from a pool essentially equal to $n$).
Near-exhaustive resampling reduces variance in the bootstrap estimate and
may inflate Cohen's $d$ for lifetime features; the mean lifetime and
max lifetime effects ($d = +3.51$, $d = +1.98$) should therefore be
interpreted as upper bounds pending replication in a larger calprotectin-stratified
sample.

Clinical activity scores corroborate this picture.
Active Crohn's (HBI $\geq 5$, $n = 93$) shows lower loop count, entropy,
and total persistence than CD remission (HBI $< 5$, $n = 457$; 4/6 features
FDR-significant; $d$ range $-0.43$ to $-0.62$).
Active UC (SCCAI $\geq 3$, $n = 114$) shows 5/6 features FDR-significant
versus UC remission (SCCAI $< 3$, $n = 240$).
Together, these results establish that topological simplification tracks not
only disease diagnosis but also continuous inflammation severity within both
CD and UC subtypes.

\subsection{Leave-one-out attribution identifies taxon-specific topological anchors}
\label{subsec:attribution}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

To identify which individual taxa anchor co-occurrence loops in healthy versus
\ibd\ microbiomes, we performed a leave-one-out (LOO) topological attribution
analysis.
For each of the 80 \clr-transformed taxa, we removed that taxon from the
group-level Spearman correlation matrix and re-ran Ripser on the resulting
$79 \times 79$ distance matrix.
The difference between the full-group and reduced-group H$_1$ feature vectors
(baseline $-$ \loo) defines each taxon's \emph{topological impact}.
A differential composite impact score---the mean of six z-scored per-feature
impact differences (healthy minus \ibd)---quantifies which taxa are
disproportionately critical to healthy loop structure.
Statistical significance was assessed by 500 label-permutation tests
(all 3,249 samples shuffled between groups, preserving group sizes);
95\% bootstrap confidence intervals were estimated from 200 within-group
resamples.
The full pipeline is implemented in \texttt{scripts/run\_loop\_attribution.py}
(AGP) and \texttt{scripts/run\_ibdmdb\_loop\_attribution.py} (IBDMDB).

\subsubsection*{AGP: \textit{Oscillospira} and \textit{Blautia} as dominant
healthy anchors (16S)}

In the AGP 16S dataset, \textit{Oscillospira} ranked first among 80 candidate
taxa (composite impact = 4.08, permutation $p = 0.006$,
95\% CI $[0.24,\,4.16]$): removing this genus from the healthy correlation
matrix produced a substantially larger reduction in H$_1$ features than
removing it from the \ibd\ matrix, indicating that \textit{Oscillospira}
participates in correlation triangles present in healthy but not \ibd\
microbiomes.
\textit{Blautia} ranked second (composite = 1.28, $p < 0.002$).
Two higher-order Clostridia taxa also reached significance:
\textit{Clostridiaceae} (rank 4, $p = 0.028$) and
\textit{Lachnospiraceae} (rank 5, $p = 0.046$).
\textit{Bacteroides} appeared at ranks 7--8, below the permutation
significance threshold in this dataset.

\subsubsection*{IBDMDB: \textit{Bacteroidetes} species as dominant healthy

Applying the same \loo\ pipeline to the IBDMDB WGS dataset
($n_\text{nonIBD} = 364$, $n_\text{IBD} = 974$; top-80 MetaPhlAn2 species)
yielded a complementary anchor ranking.
\textit{Bacteroides finegoldii} ranked first
(composite = 1.95, $p < 0.002$, 95\% CI $[0.30,\,2.23]$);
\textit{Bacteroides thetaiotaomicron} ranked third ($p = 0.018$).
Among Firmicutes, \textit{Flavonifractor plautii} (rank 10, $p = 0.014$)
and \textit{Clostridium nexile} (rank 12, $p = 0.034$) were also

At the genus level, 7 of the 20 top IBDMDB anchors matched genera present in
the AGP top 20 (Figure~\ref{fig:ibdmdb_overlap}).

The Firmicutes-dominated AGP ranking versus the Bacteroidetes-dominated IBDMDB
ranking is consistent with known 16S amplicon limitations in resolving

Sections~\ref{subsec:ibd_results}--\ref{subsec:ibdmdb}.

\paragraph{Results.}
Four observations stand out (Table~\ref{tab:classification},
Figure~\ref{fig:classification_roc}).

\textit{Aitchison-PCoA is the strongest single predictor.}
Aitchison-PCoA(10) achieves LR AUC $= 0.734$ and RF AUC $= 0.703$,
outperforming Shannon diversity (LR: $0.688$; RF: $0.563$) and topology
scalars alone (LR: $0.668$; RF: $0.632$).
Aitchison distance captures the full pairwise co-occurrence geometry of all
80 taxa simultaneously; Shannon is a per-sample marginal summary and the six
H$_1$ scalars compress persistence diagrams into coarse statistics.

\textit{Bray--Curtis PCoA performs comparably to Shannon.}
BC-PCoA(10) achieves LR: $0.683$; RF: $0.599$, substantially below
Aitchison-PCoA.
This is consistent with theoretical expectations: Aitchison distance is
the natural metric for compositional data, while Bray--Curtis on relative
abundances lacks the log-ratio invariance that makes Aitchison distance
appropriate for CLR-transformed microbiome data.

\textit{Topology scalars do not improve over Aitchison when combined.}
Aitchison-PCoA + Topology achieves LR: $0.728$, RF: $0.696$---both slightly
below Aitchison alone ($0.734$, $0.703$).
The full combination (Aitchison + Topology + Shannon) reaches LR: $0.735$
($+0.001$ over Aitchison alone, within a single SD of $\pm 0.022$).
This indicates that the structural information captured by the six H$_1$
scalars is largely subsumed by the full distance geometry that Aitchison PCoA
encodes.

\textit{Topology scalars outperform Shannon under random forest.}
RF AUC: Topology $0.632$ vs.\ Shannon $0.563$.
The relational, multi-taxon nature of topological features provides
discriminative signal that single-sample diversity indices cannot access.

\paragraph{Predictive vs.\ interpretive value.}
These results distinguish two types of contribution.
Predictively, Aitchison beta-diversity (10 PCoA axes, logistic regression,
AUC $= 0.734$) is the strongest feature set tested.
The six H$_1$ scalars do not add over this baseline, reflecting their coarse
compression of the full persistence diagram.
Interpretively, topology provides what PCoA coordinates do not: identification
of \emph{which structural property} of the co-occurrence graph is lost in
\ibd\ (loops, not merely taxon abundances), attribution of loop loss to
specific taxa (Section~\ref{subsec:attribution}), and tracking of topological
simplification against a continuous inflammation biomarker
(Section~\ref{subsec:ibdmdb}).
These mechanistic properties cannot be recovered from a PCoA embedding, even
one with superior predictive AUC.
The classification benchmark quantifies information content as a secondary
validation; it does not exhaust the analytical value of topology.

\paragraph{Precision-recall analysis and threshold-dependent metrics.}
AUROC summarises ranking quality but does not answer the sharper question of
false-positive behaviour, which matters acutely when the positive class (IBD)
is rare.
At a prevalence of $5.1\%$ ($165 / 3{,}249$), the precision-recall (PR) curve
and its area (AP; average precision) are the appropriate summary statistics.
We evaluate logistic-regression classifiers at two operating points:
\textbf{A)}~threshold $= 0.50$ and
\textbf{B)}~a fold-specific threshold calibrated to achieve
$\geq 95\%$ specificity on the training fold, then applied to the held-out
test fold (no leakage).
Results are shown in Table~\ref{tab:pr_metrics} and
Figure~\ref{fig:pr_curves}.

At the standard threshold ($t = 0.50$), all models show high recall but low
precision because balanced class weights shift probability mass toward the
positive class.
Aitchison-PCoA achieves the highest recall ($0.727$) and F1 ($0.171$);
topology-only has the lowest precision ($0.076$), reflecting that the
coarse six-dimensional feature space provides a less decisive boundary than
the full distance geometry of Aitchison PCoA.

At the 95\%-specificity operating point, only 5\% of healthy individuals are
mis-labelled as IBD regardless of classifier.
Under this constraint, the maximum achievable precision is fundamentally
bounded by prevalence (Bayes limit); with $p = 0.051$ and $\text{FPR} = 0.05$,
even a perfect-sensitivity classifier yields precision $\approx 0.51$.
In practice, Aitchison-PCoA reaches recall $= 0.182$, precision $= 0.155$;
the best combination (Aitchison + Topology + Shannon) reaches recall $= 0.194$,
precision $= 0.158$.
Critically, \textbf{topology scalars alone perform worst at this operating
point} (recall $= 0.121$, precision $= 0.118$), below both Shannon and all
$\beta$-diversity embeddings.
The hypothesis that topology might be unusually precise at conservative
thresholds is not supported: at 95\% specificity, topology identifies the
fewest IBD cases with the lowest precision of any model tested.

AP scores (area under the full PR curve) follow the same ordering as AUROC:
Aitchison + Topo + Shannon ($0.136$) $>$ Aitchison-PCoA ($0.128$) $>$
Shannon ($0.116$) $>$ BC-PCoA ($0.093$) $>$ Topology ($0.089$),
compared to a no-skill baseline of $0.051$.
The absolute AP values are modest across all models, confirming that
microbiome features alone are not sufficient for clinically actionable IBD
screening; the classification benchmark quantifies relative information content,
not clinical utility.

\subsection{H\textsubscript{0} fragmentation features: a complementary
  dimension of topological collapse}
\label{subsec:h0_features}
%─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

homology group $H_0$ encodes a distinct and biologically interpretable
topological property: the fragmentation of the co-occurrence network into
disconnected components.
In a healthy microbiome, the Vietoris--Rips filtration on the CLR correlation
matrix merges into a single connected component (Betti-0 $= 1$) at a
relatively low distance threshold; in a dysbiotic microbiome, isolated
correlation clusters may persist to higher thresholds before merging,
producing longer-lived $H_0$ bars (``fragmentation'').

We extracted four per-sample $H_0$ features using the same $k$-NN
neighbourhood ($k = 60$) as the $H_1$ pipeline: $H_0$ component count,
total $H_0$ persistence, mean $H_0$ lifetime, and max $H_0$ lifetime.
Table~\ref{tab:h0_classification} reports the cross-validated AUC when
these features are used alone and in combination with $H_1$ scalars
and Aitchison-PCoA.
