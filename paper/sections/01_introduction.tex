%=============================================================================
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}
%=============================================================================

Characterizing the human gut microbiome has, for two decades, meant counting
taxa and summarizing their relative proportions~\citep{hmp2012structure,
turnbaugh2007human}.
This reductionist accounting has proven clinically informative
(\textit{Faecalibacterium prausnitzii} depletion, Proteobacteria blooms,
enterotype discretization), yet it systematically discards the relational
fabric that gives a microbial community its functional identity: the web of
cross-feeding dependencies, competitive exclusions, and syntrophic partnerships
that collectively determine what a community \emph{does} rather than merely
what it \emph{contains}.
Co-occurrence networks partially recover this information by encoding pairwise
correlational structure, but their standard reductions (edge density, modularity
index, clustering coefficient) remain insensitive to higher-dimensional
topological organization: the loops, multi-node cycles, and nested connectivity
motifs that govern resilience and metabolic redundancy.

\subsection{Why topology, and why now}

Persistent homology~\citep{carlsson2009topology,edelsbrunner2008persistent}
offers a mathematically precise vocabulary for this organizational structure.
Rather than selecting a single correlation threshold and analyzing the
resulting binary network---a choice that discards information above and below
that cutoff simultaneously---Vietoris--Rips filtration tracks topological features
(\textit{connected components, loops, cavities}) as they nucleate and
extinguish across the full spectrum of correlation distances.
The resulting persistence diagram encodes both \emph{which} features appear
and \emph{how robustly} they persist, the latter being the operative
summary statistic: features that survive across a wide filtration range reflect
strong, multiply-reinforced co-occurrence relationships rather than chance
statistical fluctuations.
Persistence-derived descriptors such as Betti curves, persistence entropy,
and total persistence have demonstrated discriminative power in neuroscience,
protein folding, materials microstructure analysis, and biological aggregation
modelling~\citep{wasserman2018topological,bubenik2015statistical,ghrist2008barcodes,topaz2015topological},
but their deployment on microbial co-occurrence networks has remained
limited~\citep{camara2017topological,liao2019tmap}.

\subsection{Inflammatory bowel disease as a topological stress test}

Inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis) provides
an exceptionally well-characterized perturbation of
gut microbial ecology~\citep{petersen2014defining}.
The compositional signature is reproducible across cohorts: contracted
\textit{Lachnospiraceae} and \textit{Ruminococcaceae} abundance, reduced
butyrate-producing capacity, elevated Proteobacteria, diminished colonization
resistance~\citep{gevers2014treatment,halfvarson2017dynamics,ley2006microbial}.
What this signature does not address is whether these compositional shifts
\emph{dismantle the loop structure} of the co-occurrence network: whether
the metabolic cross-feeding circuits that butyrate producers anchor collapse
when those organisms are numerically depleted, leaving behind a topologically
impoverished interaction landscape.
This is the mechanistic hypothesis our analysis tests empirically.

\subsection{Contributions}

This paper makes seven specific claims:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*, itemsep=3pt]
  \item \textbf{Methodological:} A paired bootstrap design for comparing
  persistence diagrams at scale, using sign-flip label permutation as the null
  and a globally pre-fixed taxon set to decouple topological signal from
  feature-space selection noise (Section~\ref{sec:methods}).

  \item \textbf{Empirical:} Across 3,409 \agp\ stool samples, \ibd\ is
  associated with systematic reduction in co-occurrence loop structure---fewer
  cycles, shorter-lived cycles, lower peak cycle density---with effect sizes
  ($d = 0.75$--$2.38$) large enough to be unambiguous
  (Section~\ref{subsec:ibd_results}).

  \item \textbf{Robustness:} The \ibd\ finding survives covariate matching
  on age, sex, and BMI and is statistically indistinguishable across taxon-set
  sizes $N \in \{50, 80, 120\}$, ruling out demographic confounding,
  taxa-selection artefact, and numerical instability as alternative
  explanations (Section~\ref{subsec:sensitivity}).

  \item \textbf{Cross-platform replication:} The \ibd\ topological-simplification
  signal replicates in the IBDMDB multi-omics cohort using whole-genome
  shotgun sequencing---a different platform, different population, and
  independent preprocessing pipeline (Section~\ref{subsec:ibdmdb}).

  \item \textbf{Biomarker grounding:} Topological features correlate with
  fecal calprotectin and clinical activity scores (HBI, SCCAI) in IBDMDB,
  linking loop collapse to a continuous, objective inflammation marker rather
  than a binary diagnostic label (Section~\ref{subsec:ibdmdb}).

  \item \textbf{Taxon-level attribution:} A leave-one-out pipeline identifies
  \textit{Oscillospira} and \textit{Blautia} as dominant loop anchors in
  healthy \agp\ networks, with \textit{Bacteroides}-clade species replicated
  as top anchors in IBDMDB WGS data, providing named biological candidates for
  the observed loop collapse (Section~\ref{subsec:attribution}).

  \item \textbf{Classification benchmark:} Logistic-regression classifiers
  using topological features alone achieve AUC $= 0.67$ for \ibd\ prediction;
  combined with Shannon diversity the AUC rises to $0.70$, confirming that
  topology carries non-redundant discriminative information relative to
  conventional alpha diversity (Section~\ref{subsec:classification}).
\end{enumerate}

\noindent
\textbf{Calibration note.} A pre-specified FDR policy (18-test
Benjamini--Hochberg correction, sign-flip primary $p$-values only, full and
matched subsets corrected independently) is stated before any result is
examined (Section~\ref{subsec:stats}).

The paper proceeds as follows.
Section~\ref{sec:methods} details dataset provenance, preprocessing, network
construction, the persistent homology pipeline, and the full statistical
framework.
Section~\ref{sec:results} reports findings for \ibd, diet, antibiotic recency,
IBDMDB replication, calprotectin grounding, taxon attribution, and the
classification benchmark.
Section~\ref{sec:discussion} situates the \ibd\ result in the IBD biology
literature, qualifies the diet finding, argues for restraint regarding
antibiotics, and enumerates the study's limitations without euphemism.
Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} closes with a minimal interpretation and a
specific agenda for genuinely open follow-on work.
